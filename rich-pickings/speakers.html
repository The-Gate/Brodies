Speakers

Bill Drummond

Managing Partner
Brodies LLP
Chairman of SCDI

Re-elected in November 2012 as Managing Partner for a sixth consecutive term, Bill has led Brodies since 1998 to become one of the largest and most successful law firms in Scotland, servicing public and private sector clients across all key areas of the Scottish economy. Ranked top tier in 21 service areas by the leading independent legal directories, Brodies has around 540 lawyers and support staff, all based in Scotland. The firm has won numerous awards for legal expertise, innovation and business performance, and last year was named the Scotland Law Firm of the Year 2013 by Who's Who Legal in its annual awards for the fourth time. In 2013 Bill was named as the UK Management Partner of the Year at the Legal Business Awards. Bill's personal commitment to Scottish economic progress is illustrated by his service as an SCDI Executive Committee and/or Board member for the past eight years. He was elected Chair of SCDI in February 2013.

Christine Tacon CBE

Groceries Code Adjudicator
Former MD of the Co-operative Farms

Christine Tacon has a portfolio career combining her commercial expertise with her understanding of the food chain and the public sector. She is a Chartered Engineer with 12 years' experience in sales and marketing of fast moving consumer goods (Mars, Anchor and Vodafone) and ran the Co-operative Group’s farming business, the largest in the UK, for 11 years until 2012. She was awarded a CBE for services to agriculture in 2004.

Fiona Richmond

Project Manager
Scotland Food & Drink

Fiona has been actively promoting and supporting quality food and drink production for more than 10 years. She has worked with industry leadership body Scotland Food & Drink since 2009, firstly helping producers grow their business in foodservice and now managing a 2014 project designed to maximise the opportunities for the food and drink sector arising from the major events in 2014. She previously worked with the Slow Food movement, including a period in the international headquarters in Italy, co-ordinating the membership and projects in the UK, and remains an active committee member with the Edinburgh group. In addition, she is a member of the Soil Association and has recently been appointed to the national Food for Life Catering Mark standards committee, and is also a member of Edinburgh’s initiative to make the capital Scotland’s first Sustainable Food City.

With an honours degree in Sociology and a Masters in Social Research Methods, she has held posts in the public and voluntary sectors including the Jersey Government. She is passionate about Scottish food and drink culture and preserving the country's superb larder and rich culinary traditions.

Jim McLaren

Chairman
Quality Meat Scotland (QMS)

Jim is currently Chairman of Quality Meat Scotland (QMS) the Scottish Red Meat Levy Body. He is a Director of NFU Mutual (he was President of NFUS from 2007 to 2011) and a founder Director of Angus Cereals, the 45,000 tonne farmer-owned grain drying and storage facility built in 2011 at Montrose Harbour. Jim is a mixed arable and beef farmer, with a herd of Pedigree Simmentals. His arable unit, growing winter wheat, spring barley and land let out for potatoes, extends to 1000 acres over four units in Perthshire. The land tenure comprises a mix of a full tenancy, a limited partnership, a Short Limited Duration Tenancy (SLDT) and owner-occupied land.

John Noble

Director
British Brands Group

John Noble is Director of the British Brands Group, a membership organisation that provides the collective voice for brand manufacturers in the UK. The Group was founded in 1994 to promote the values and benefits of brands to consumers, the economy and society, as well as to represent brand manufacturers collectively on policy issues. Through its lectures, research studies, conferences, publications and briefings, the British Brands Group is committed to deepening understanding among those who make and shape public policy of the contribution of brands to the quality of our everyday lives and the success of the economy. It is part of a network of similar organisations across Europe and represents AIM, the European Brands Association, in the UK.

John is a Chartered Marketer and, prior to joining the British Brands Group, followed a marketing career that started with British Airways before moving into marketing consultancy with Marketing Solutions. He then became Head of Marketing for Hamptons, the property firm, and held a senior marketing role with Bristol & West International.

Mark Simmers

Chief Executive Officer
Celtic Renewables

Mark Simmers is CEO of Celtic Renewables, a start-up business in the renewable energy from waste sector. Celtic Renewables' innovative technology converts low value organic wastes and residues into high value chemicals and other products, providing industries with a complete residue disposal solution by producing high value commodities from sustainable and low value inputs. The initial focus of the company’s patented and innovative fermentation process is the by-products of malt whisky production (draft and pot ale) which can be converted into high value products: Biobutanol (a direct replacement for petrol); Acetone; Bioethanol; and high-protein animal feed. The company has plans to build the first large scale bio-refinery in Scotland in 2015/16, which will herald the development of a new sustainable industry in Scotland. The potential market in Scotland alone is worth over £100million, and the technology has global potential.

Mark is a natural leader and an entrepreneur with global experience in commercialisation, sales and project management. In 2011 Mark graduated as a Saltire Foundation Fellow, having completed the 12 month Global Entrepreneurial Leadership Programme. Prior to the Saltire Foundation, Mark spent 20 years in the Sports Marketing sector.

Bruce Davidson

Managing Director
Loch Fyne Oysters

Bruce has been MD of Loch Fyne Oysters since 2006. Loch Fyne has been cultivating oysters and mussels and smoking salmon at the head of the Loch for over 30 years. The site also hosts the popular Loch Fyne Oyster Bar and Deli which attract consumers from both the UK and overseas throughout the year.

Bruce returned to Scotland to take up the Loch Fyne role having spent the rest of his career outside Scotland. Between 1997 and 2006 he was on the Board of Imperial Tobacco as Group Sales and Marketing Director. Prior to this he lived in Singapore as CEO Asia Pacific for Timberland for five years, covering 13 markets across this region.

His early career was dominated by British American Tobacco, joining as a management trainee and working in various sales and marketing roles in the Middle East and Far East over a 12-year period.

Bruce was educated at Morrison’s Academy, Crieff, and at the University of Edinburgh, where he studied for a Bachelor of Commerce degree.

Bruce Ferguson

Scottish General Manager
Openfield

Bruce is the Scottish General Manager for Openfield and is also Managing Director of both Aberdeen Grain & Angus Cereals. He is also currently Chairman of the AIC’s Scottish Arable Committee.

Bruce has been hugely influential in developing the Aberdeen Grain business as well as establishing the new cooperative Angus Cereals in 2010, in total both businesses are now handling collectively over 115,000 tonnes. His background has always been farming and he was born and brought up on a mixed family farm at Alyth, Perthshire. Bruce has been involved in the grain business since 1988 working with Scottish Agricultural Industries initially, followed by Allied Grain, Dalgety and finally Grainfarmers/Openfield. He gained an MBA (Distinction) in 1998 after four years of part time study.

Alasdair Gardner

Managing Director of Commercial Banking
Bank of Scotland

Appointed to his current role in January 2013, Alasdair leads the commercial business in Scotland. This involves customers whose turnover is in excess £1million ~ £750million supporting them with a full range of banking services from debt serving and capital markets to deposit products. He also sits on the Scottish Executive Committee and is a Trustee the Bank of Scotland Foundation.

The main focus of Alasdair’s role is supporting the Scottish economy through the provision of a full range of banking services to businesses across the whole of Scotland.  He also sits on the Scottish Government’s Scottish Business Board and the SCDI Executive Committee.

Prior to his current role he held various senior roles in both the renewable energy and oil and gas sectors and was Head of Energy for HBOS. He has also gained substantial experience operating in the leverage debt market. A graduate of Edinburgh University he is also a Fellow of the Chartered Institute of Bankers Scotland.

Alasdair is responsible for 120 relationship directors throughout the whole of the Scottish footprint with three Mid Markets area offices in Edinburgh, Aberdeen and Glasgow, and 15 offices under SME.
