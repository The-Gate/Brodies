Key contacts

Eric Galbraith

Partner, Brodies LLP

Phone:

0141 245 6253

Email:

eric.galbraith@brodies.com

Eric brings a wealth of specialist international expertise to the corporate/M&A team. He is widely regarded as a leading lawyer in his specialist field, having built a formidable reputation for delivering on international projects on behalf of clients.

A corporate lawyer of nearly 30 years' standing, Eric specialises in mergers & acquisitions, joint ventures and international franchising, regularly advising clients in the food and drink industry and in the public sector on corporate structures, with an emphasis on charities and social enterprises.

Chambers UK independent legal directory have commended Eric as a lawyer who "genuinely has his clients' interests at heart", “is very accurate and understands situations rapidly,” and “always comes up with the right solution."

Odell Milne

Partner, Brodies LLP

Phone:

0131 656 0189

Email:

odell.milne@brodies.com

Odell Milne heads Brodies' Agricultural and Estates team. She has more than twenty years' experience in rural and estate business and is accredited as a Specialist in Agricultural Law by the Law Society of Scotland.

Odell provides advice to estates and family businesses across Scotland in relation to succession planning and partnership matters; land reform; agricultural tenancies; commercial development, mineral agreements, and wind and hydro schemes.

Odell is involved in transactions covering the full range of rural property matters including the sale and purchase of estates, farms, salmon fishings and forestry.

Author of a leading textbook on compulsory purchase, Odell is recognised as a specialist in compulsory purchase and utility wayleave agreements.

Named as Property/Farm Adviser of the Year in the Scottish Farmer Supplier Awards in June 2013, she was commended for her skill at cutting through jargon and delivering concise, practical advice to the wide range of clients she represents in the rural sector.

Odell is ranked by The Legal 500 and Chambers & Partners for both agricultural law and transport.

Victoria Moore

Associate, Brodies LLP

Phone:

0141 245 6740

Email:

victoria.moore@brodies.com

Victoria is a member of the Intellectual Property, Technology and Outsourcing team advising on a wide range of commercial contracts, with particular emphasis on those involving technology and intellectual property. She advises on the licensing and ownership of intellectual property, procurement of content, brands and IT systems, maintenance and support arrangements, agency and distribution agreements, and franchise arrangements.

Victoria works for clients in a variety of sectors from online businesses, drinks companies and travel operators to financial institutions, she is an active member of both the firm's Food & Drink and Aerospace & Defence groups. Her experience to date includes drafting, advising on and negotiation of standard form and complex supply and distribution arrangements, website / software development, services contracts, maintenance and hosting, warehousing arrangements, content acquisition and licensing.

Victoria, is dual-qualified in Scotland and England, has spent a number of years working in both private practice (for a technology focused international firm) and in-house (in a diverse range of business sectors) in London for domestic and international clients and as a result has a solid appreciation of the intricacies of the commercial and political demands on businesses and the need for practical solutions and bespoke legal drafting.

Clive Phillips

Partner, Brodies LLP

Phone:

01224 392 281

Email:

clive.phillips@brodies.com

Clive has an in depth knowledge of the rural sector both as a specialist in agricultural law and also having a family farm business himself. He acts for a wide range of landed estates and farming clients with an emphasis on commercial farms and estates with diversified interests. He is particularly interested in farm business structures and opportunities for those businesses in the food supply chain.

He is instructed regularly on diversification projects, particularly wind farms, AD plants and other renewable energy projects, with particular experience with options, leases and joint venture arrangements.

His understanding of the interaction of land ownership, rural business and succession enables Clive to advise effectively on structuring farming partnerships and other rural businesses in order to provide strategic succession planning advice.

Clive is regularly instructed to troubleshoot disputed agricultural holdings and other contentious situations in the rural sector.

Clive is ranked by Chambers & Partners in agriculture and rural affairs.
