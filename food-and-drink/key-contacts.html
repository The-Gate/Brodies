Key contacts

Eric Galbraith

Partner, Head of Food & Drink, Brodies LLP

Phone:

+44 (0)141 245 6253

Email:

eric.galbraith@brodies.com

Eric brings a wealth of specialist international expertise to the corporate/M&A team. He is widely regarded as a leading lawyer in his specialist field, having built a formidable reputation for delivering on international projects on behalf of clients.

A corporate lawyer of nearly 30 years' standing, Eric specialises in mergers & acquisitions, joint ventures and international franchising, regularly advising clients in the food and drink industry and in the public sector on corporate structures, with an emphasis on charities and social enterprises.

Chambers UK independent legal directory have commended Eric as a lawyer who "genuinely has his clients' interests at heart", "is very accurate and understands situations rapidly," and "always comes up with the right solution."

Christine O'Neill

Partner & Chairman, Brodies LLP

Phone:

+44 (0)131 656 0286

Email:

christine.oneill@brodies.com

Christine O'Neill is Chairman of Brodies LLP and a recognised expert on public and constitutional law issues in Scotland. She is, uniquely in Scotland, rated by Chambers and Partners as a Star Individual in administrative and public law and is also ranked in Band 1 for parliamentary and public affairs. Her hallmark is developing strong client relationships in the public and private sectors and providing clear, practical advice.

Christine advises clients on legislation and statutory interpretation, the powers and duties of public bodies including the Scottish Parliament, and freedom of information laws and data protection. She acts for a range of Scottish and UK clients including BBC Scotland, the Gambling Commission, the Scottish Courts Service, the Scottish Qualifications Authority, and a range of local authorities.

Recognised for her experience and expertise in handling litigation, Christine is regularly instructed in judicial reviews, statutory appeals and public inquiries. She currently leads the team acting for the Scotch Whisky Association and European drinks producers in their challenge to the Scottish Parliament's legislation on minimum pricing of alcohol.

Charles Livingstone

Partner, Public Law and Regulatory, Brodies LLP

Phone:

+44 (0)141 248 4672

Email:

charles.livingstone@brodies.com

Charles works with a broad range of commercial, public sector, charitable and individual clients, advising them on public law issues including judicial review, human rights, information law and the powers and duties of local and other public authorities. He regularly advises clients on constitutional issues such as the limits on the Scottish Parliament's powers, and has significant experience of delivering private legislation including Harbour Revision Orders and Acts of the Scottish Parliament. He was one of the most in-demand commentators on the legal and constitutional questions raised by the Scottish independence debate.

Charles also has substantial competition law expertise, advising clients on issues including antitrust compliance and investigations, merger control, State aid and public procurement.

Charles is an experienced litigator, having handled major cases in the Sheriff courts, the Court of Session, the UK Supreme Court and House of Lords, and the Court of Justice of the European Union.

Clive Phillips

Partner, Land & Rural Business, Brodies LLP

Phone:

+44 (0)1224 392 281

Email:

clive.phillips@brodies.com

Clive has an in depth knowledge of the rural sector as a specialist in agricultural law and also having a family farm business as a landowner and tenant himself. Clive acts for a wide range of landed estates and farming clients with an emphasis on commercial farms and estates with diversified interests. He advises on farm and estate acquisitions and sale, agricultural leases, sporting rights and farming partnerships.

Understanding the interaction of land ownership, rural business and succession enables Clive to advise effectively on structuring farming partnerships and other rural businesses in order to provide strategic succession planning advice. He is regularly instructed to troubleshoot disputed agricultural holdings and other contentious situations in the rural sector, or to assist other firms of solicitors with such situations.
